Shakshouka With Buns

From Ziv, slightly reformatted by me.

Shakshouka recipe (8 eggs):

Using a big frying/cooking pan.

  1. Fry (with olive oil) 2 shredded onions (add salt& pepper)
  2. After 2 minutes add ~3-8 shredded garlic cloves (I use 8).
  3. After 3 minutes add 8 shredded tomatoes.
  4. Cook for 5-10 minutes.
  5. Add ~1 cup of tomato-paste ( + optional can of tomato-sauce .)
  6. Season with salt, pepper, oregano, sweet paprika, spicy paprika (a lot of spicy paprika).
  7. Reduce the heat to low-medium, put a cover, and cook for 40 minutes or so.

Buns+shakshouka:

  • 500 grams (yes grams, deal with it ðŸ˜‰ ) flour
  • 1 table spoon of dry yeast
  • 4 table spoons of sugar
  • 1 table spoon of shredded garlic (optional)
  • 1 large egg
  • 0.25 cup of olive oil
  • 1.25 water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • In a stand mixer (a real one, not the impressions one), put all ingredients together (except the water) and start it.
  • After 2 minute add the salt.
  • After about 10 minutes of operations, move the dough into a large bowl, cover with a towel, and let it swell (it should double the original size).
  • Make dough balls and place it on the perimeter of the cooking pan (like a clock).
  • Spread some egg on top of the buns and put sesame seeds. The dough is going to swell in the oven.
  • Put the pre-prepared shakshouka in the middle and put inside the oven (190 degrees celsius ðŸ˜‰ ).
  • After 10 minutes move it out and add eggs (as much as can).
  • Insert it back to the oven for about 10 minutes more (or until the eggs is ready as you wish).
